Types of Mangoes

Ataulfo Mango

The Ataulfo mango comes from Mexico, and is named after Ataulfo Morales Gordillo, who first grew it. Mangoes are thought to have arrived in Mexico via Brazil, where they were brought by Portuguese travellers in the 1700s.

Alphonso Mango

Smooth and creamy with a soft skin of deep marigold, Alphonso mangoes are considered to be the pinnacle of mangoes, with a superior taste, flavour, texture and sensation to all others.

Irwin Mango

Irwin fruit is of ovate shape, with a rounded base and a pointed apex, lacking a beak. The smooth skin develops an eye-catching dark red blush at maturity. The flesh is yellow and has a mild but sweet flavor and a pleasant aroma.

Keitt Mango

Keitt mangos are popular in Asian cultures, where they are enjoyed in its mature-green stage or even as pickles. Primarily sourced from Mexico, Ecuador, Brazil and here in the United States - the Keitt varietal has peak availability from March to April and from August to September.

Amarpalli

The Amrapali mango is revered for its rich and aromatic taste, as its sweetness takes center stage. It is like a love letter to your taste buds, captivating you with its irresistible charm and exquisite taste.

Girkesar

The 'Gir Kesar' mango, also called Kesar, is a mango cultivar grown in the foothills of Girnar in Gujarat, western India. The mango is known for its bright orange colored pulp and was given the geographical indication status in 2011.
